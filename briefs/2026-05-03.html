Syria

May 2, 2026 · Security High impact

Sipan Hamo confirms four SDF brigades have integrated into the Syrian Defense Ministry under the new eastern command

Sipan Hamo, who took the eastern-region brief inside the Defense Ministry as Samir Oso, confirmed that four Syrian Democratic Forces brigades have folded into the new Syrian army structure under the January 29 Damascus-SDF agreement, with the brigades positioned in Ain al-Arab, Hasakah, Qamishli and Malikiyah and the Eastern Region command covering Hasakah, Raqqa and Deir Ezzor. Hamo flagged the integration as still early-stage and noted that detained-fighter releases promised in the original deal remain unfulfilled while the final divisional structure waits on a new regulatory framework.

May 2, 2026 · Energy High impact

Seventy Iraqi crude tankers cross at Rabia-al-Yarubiyah for Banyas, Syria opens itself as a Hormuz alternative export route

Syrian Petroleum Company communications director Safwan Sheikh Ahmad confirmed that 70 Iraqi crude tankers reached the Rabia border en route to Syria via the al-Yarubiyah crossing, then up the M4 highway to the Mediterranean export terminal at Banyas. The shipments were rerouted from al-Tanf to al-Yarubiyah specifically to shorten transit time, and the General Company for Iraqi Ports announced Friday the official start of crude exports through the Rabia corridor, converting an emergency war-time workaround into a live alternative to the still-blocked Strait of Hormuz.

Central Bank extends the Syrian pound exchange window to June 30 to keep the new banknote rollout on track

The Central Bank pushed the deadline for citizens to swap out the old Syrian pound for the new redenominated banknotes that entered circulation on January 1 to June 30, 2026, giving households and informal-economy holders an extra two months to migrate. The extension takes pressure off the conversion bottleneck and keeps the Husrieh redenomination program inside its political envelope without forcing the CBS to absorb a hard cliff in counter exchanges.

Iraq

May 2, 2026 · Banking High impact

USD 500 million cash dollar shipment lands in Baghdad on May 1, ending a two-month CBI pause

Iraq received around USD 500 million in cash dollars on May 1 in the first such shipment in over two months, restoring the roughly USD 1 billion-a-month cash flow that runs in two tranches. PM Sudani's financial advisor Mazhar Mohammed Saleh framed the resumption as a logistics issue tied to the February 28 airspace closure, now reopened after the early-April ceasefire, while economist Nabil al-Marsoumi flagged the shipment as a direct support to the Iraqi dinar.

May 2, 2026 · Banking

CBI Governor al-Allaq says transfers now run at 95 percent of dollar sales as cash imports drop from USD 14 billion to USD 4 billion a year

CBI Governor Ali al-Allaq confirmed dollar transfers to Baghdad continue normally despite the oil-export halt, with electronic transfers now accounting for around 95 percent of total dollar sales after the bank shifted on-boarded clients to AML-compliant rails and cut annual cash imports from roughly USD 14 billion to about USD 4 billion. Al-Allaq also said public-sector salaries remain protected and that there are no restrictions on foreign-currency transfers or sales for trade and travel.

Oil Ministry Undersecretary Bassem Mohammed maps a Basra-Haditha pipeline and the al-Waleed and al-Rabia crossings as the Hormuz workaround

Oil Ministry Undersecretary Bassem Mohammed framed Iraq's export reset around a proposed Basra-Haditha pipeline and the April-opened border crossings at al-Waleed in Anbar and al-Rabia in Nineveh, while flagging that current exports run only at about 200,000 bpd via Ceyhan plus limited fuel-oil shipments through Syria. State Department clears Kuwait for a USD 2.5 billion integrated battle-command system inside an emergency USD 8.6 billion regional package

Secretary of State Marco Rubio invoked emergency authority to bypass standard congressional review and clear an USD 8.6 billion arms package for the region, with Kuwait approved for a USD 2.5 billion integrated battle-command system designed to coordinate the country's air-defense and missile-defense layers. The same package puts USD 4.01 billion of Patriot defense and USD 992.4 million of APKWS through to Qatar, USD 992.4 million of APKWS to Israel and USD 147.6 million of APKWS to the UAE, framing Kuwait's command-and-control buy as the largest single line outside the Patriot tranche.

PAM opens private-sector visa transfer window with 676 first-day applications and a June 30 deadline

The Public Authority for Manpower opened the new expat visa-transfer window on Friday May 1, 2026, with 676 applications received on the first day and a hard deadline of June 30 for workers to migrate from current employers into SME, industrial, agricultural, livestock and fishing sectors via the Ashal Companies online service. PAM described the program as a flexible regulatory step to balance the labour market against employer interests, with the original employer's approval still required.
