Syria

Apr 26, 2026 · Justice

First public trial of an Assad-era official opens in Damascus with Atef Najib in the dock, May 10 next session

The preparatory session of the trial of Atef Najib, the former head of political security in Daraa whose 2011 detention and torture of a group of teenagers triggered the uprising, opened Sunday in Damascus with Najib appearing as the sole defendant on charges of crimes against the Syrian people. The court adjourned to Sunday May 10 for the second session, making this the first formal public trial of a deposed-regime figure since the December 2024 transition and the operational sequel to last week's Tadamon arrest and Daraa case-files announcement.

Sharaa frames justice as a supreme objective of state institutions, anchors trial calendar to victims' file

President Ahmad al-Sharaa said Sunday that justice will stand as one of the highest values upheld by the Syrian people and a supreme objective the state and its institutions are committed to delivering, framing the courtroom track in Damascus as essential to repairing civil peace and pursuing those responsible for the suffering inflicted on the Syrian people. Syrian Investment Authority closes drafting work on first International Arbitration Center, formal launch imminent

SANA reported Sunday that preparatory work on Syria's first International Arbitration Center is almost complete with only final procedural steps remaining, the institution to handle investment, commercial, civil, economic and cross-border arbitration cases under international standards. The 11-member drafting committee bringing together Syrian, Emirati, Lebanese, Saudi, Qatari and Jordanian legal specialists has finished its mandate, and officials say the founding law is expected in the near future. Translated from Arabic.

Apr 26, 2026 · Energy

Hormuz transit recovers to 19 vessels on April 25 as US extends interception campaign into the Arabian Sea

Maritime intelligence tracked 19 transits through the Strait of Hormuz on April 25 (five inbound, 14 outbound), the strongest day-on-day rebound since the late-March stoppage, while US naval forces intercepted the Panama-flagged LPG tanker LPG SEVAN in the central Arabian Sea as part of a widened sanctions enforcement footprint. Apr 27, 2026 · Aviation

Kuwait Airways adds Beirut, Cairo daily, Trivandrum, Dhaka and Colombo to the network from Monday

Kuwait Airways extended the post-war restart Monday with daily Cairo service, twice-weekly Beirut on Mondays and Wednesdays, twice-weekly Trivandrum on Mondays and Fridays, four-weekly Dhaka and a weekly Colombo line, lifting the cumulative restored network past 13 destinations and onto track for the previously announced 35-destination weekly tempo. The Monday additions are the first incremental schedule prints since Sunday's headline 73-flight twin-airline launch and signal a steady-state ramp rather than a one-shot reopening.

Apr 26, 2026 · Fiscal

Finance Ministry submits FY26-27 budget at KD 26.1bn spend and KD 9.8bn deficit, capex set at KD 3.1bn

The Finance Ministry's FY26-27 budget submission to the Diwan came in at KD 26.1bn in expenditure against KD 16.3bn in projected revenue, leaving a KD 9.8bn deficit, a 54.7 percent widening over the prior year and the largest gap since the 2020 oil shock. Wages and subsidies absorb KD 19.8bn (76 percent of spend), capex sits at KD 3.1bn (11.8 percent), with Kuwait International Airport expansion, Mubarak Al-Kabeer Port, North Kabd wastewater and Subbiya power Phase 2 named in the major-projects schedule.

Apr 24, 2026 · Banking sector

NBK reports KD 135.5m Q1 net profit, total assets cross KD 46bn as loan book grows 11 percent

National Bank of Kuwait booked Q1 2026 net profit of KD 135.5m (444m dollars), up 1.0 percent year on year and well above the KD 120.8m consensus, with operating income up 6.6 percent to KD 331.2m, total assets up 10.7 percent to KD 46.1bn, and group loans up 10.9 percent to KD 27.3bn. Boubyan Bank cleared a parallel KD 26.4m print, while Q1 foreign investment into the sector tracked 6 percent lower at 14.9bn dollars, the first sequential decline since the late-2024 reform package landed.

Apr 26, 2026 · Oil & Gas

KRG pipeline to Ceyhan slated to lift to 330,000 bpd as Basra crude joins the blend

The KRG Ministry of Natural Resources said exports through the Kurdistan Region pipeline to Ceyhan are set to climb to 330,000 bpd once Basra crude formally joins the Kirkuk and KRG-produced blend, a 30 percent lift over the current ~250,000 bpd run rate and the largest northern-route throughput since pre-2023. The blending agreement converts the pipeline from a Kirkuk-and-KRG export channel into a federal-route override against Hormuz disruption, embedding the KRG more deeply into Baghdad's headline export mix.

Apr 26, 2026 · Security

Monitor records 50 strikes on KRG soil since the April 8 ceasefire, IRGC behind 75 percent

The Kurdistan Region has been targeted nearly 50 times since the US-Iran ceasefire took effect on April 8, with monitoring data attributing 75 percent of the strikes directly to the IRGC and the remaining 25 percent to Iran-aligned factions inside Iraq. The cut breaks the post-ceasefire breach pattern out of the cumulative 809-figure announced over the weekend and shows the violations are being driven from Tehran rather than from autonomous proxy groups, narrowing the diplomatic ask Erbil can credibly route through Baghdad.
