Barnieh and Faivre open Syrian-French banking track, push to reopen the Central Bank of Syria's account in France

Finance Minister Yisr Barnieh and French chargé d'affaires Jean-Baptiste Faivre held a working session in Damascus on the financing pipeline for development projects, the role of French and European institutions, and the readout from the IMF and World Bank Spring Meetings, while Central Bank Governor Abdulqader al-Hasriya separately discussed reopening the Central Bank of Syria's account in France with Faivre to reactivate direct settlement channels. Iraq

Apr 27, 2026 · Government

President Amidi tasks Ali al-Zaidi with forming the next government, ending the four-month Coordination Framework deadlock

President Nizar Amidi formally tasked Ali Falih al-Zaidi with forming the next Iraqi cabinet on Monday evening after the Coordination Framework converged on his name, with both Nouri al-Maliki and caretaker PM Mohammed Shia al-Sudani withdrawing their candidacies to clear the path. Al-Zaidi has 30 days under Article 76 to assemble a cabinet that can clear 167 votes in parliament, and the Framework statement framed the Maliki-Sudani step-down as a "historic and responsible position" that lifted the Trump-set Friday deadline pressure off the bloc.

Al-Zaidi profile reads as a banker-businessman first, a 40-year-old Al-Janoob Islamic Bank chairman with no prior office

Al-Zaidi is the chairman of Al-Janoob Islamic Bank, owner of Dijlah TV, and the operator of the supply chain feeding Iraq's universal food ration programme, with stakes spanning private banking, Islamic finance, real estate and media but no prior cabinet, ministerial or parliamentary post. Apr 27, 2026 · Energy

Hormuz transits drop to single digits as the dual US-Iran blockade keeps the strait near-closed

Bloomberg's Hormuz tracker recorded transits collapsing back to single-digit volumes against a 60-ship pre-crisis daily norm as the parallel US naval blockade and IRGC enforcement campaign keep the corridor effectively shut, with Polymarket pricing a 99.7 percent implied probability against normal traffic returning by April 30. Around 20,000 seafarers are stranded on hundreds of vessels inside the Gulf, and the IEA April Oil Market Report logged OPEC+ output 9.4 million bpd lower month on month at 42.4 million bpd, the steepest sequential drop on record.

Iran offers to reopen the Strait of Hormuz in exchange for an end to the US blockade and a freeze on the war

Tehran tabled a fresh proposal to lift its closure of the Strait of Hormuz in return for the US ending the naval blockade and the wider Iran war, with the package deferring nuclear-program negotiations to a later track, and the White House said Trump is reviewing the offer rather than rejecting it outright. The proposal lands as Iranian gunboats and US forces continue to interdict tankers inside the corridor and is the first Tehran-side framework that could in principle restart Kuwaiti and Iraqi southern lifting before any nuclear settlement.

Apr 27, 2026 · Energy

Kuwait export crude marks above 153 dollars per barrel as KPC force majeure stays in force

Kuwait Export Crude was last marked at around 153.22 dollars per barrel, roughly 10 dollars higher than the previous official print as the Hormuz dual blockade keeps the price embedded with a war premium, while KPC's force majeure on crude and refined-product shipments remains active and Kuwaiti production sits at levels not seen since the early 1990s. The headline price tape papers over a near-zero physical lifting reality, and KPC has explicitly told customers it will not be in a position to clear back-orders even after the strait reopens.

Apr 27, 2026 · Shipping

Around 20,000 seafarers stranded on hundreds of Gulf-side vessels as the Hormuz standoff enters its ninth week

Industry monitors put roughly 20,000 seafarers stuck inside the Gulf on tankers, gas carriers and bulk vessels unable to transit Hormuz, with the standoff now in its ninth week and crews reportedly running through provisioning and repatriation windows. Insurance war-risk premia for southern Iraqi and Kuwaiti lifters remain pegged at multiples of the pre-February rate, and shipping desks are quietly redrafting Q3 contracts on the assumption Hormuz traffic will not normalise before late summer.

Negotiating delegations from the KDP and PUK are scheduled to meet in Erbil on Thursday April 30 to take three linked files together: a unified Kurdish nominee for the federal Iraqi presidency, the still-blocked tenth KRG cabinet, and resumption of Kurdistan parliament work.
