Coordination Framework postpones PM decision again, three days left on April 26 constitutional clock

The Coordination Framework again postponed its PM nomination on Wednesday, with Secretary General Abbas Radi al-Amiri confirming further consultations are needed before a final pick, leaving Sudani, Bassem al-Badri, and Ihsan al-Awadi all still in frame. Apr 21, 2026 · Mega Projects

Iraqi cabinet approves Basra-Haditha crude pipeline tender with Baniyas and Aqaba branches under study

At its weekly Tuesday session the cabinet approved a multi-billion dollar tender for an 800-kilometre crude pipeline from Basra to Haditha, with technical work for extensions to Syria's Baniyas and Jordan's Aqaba moving forward. The oil ministry will issue international bid documents in the coming weeks and has framed the route as the most feasible non-Hormuz export lane, tested against the 1980s and 2022 Aqaba plans that stalled on financing.

Kirkuk-Ceyhan flow stabilises near 250,000 bpd, North Oil Co rehabs parallel line for additional capacity

Hormuz records partial restart, eight ships including three tankers transit Wednesday as IRGC keeps conditional closure

Tracking data showed eight vessels, three of them crude tankers, transiting the Strait of Hormuz on Wednesday, the first material flow since the April 18 closure, though still orders of magnitude below the pre-war 100-plus daily average.
