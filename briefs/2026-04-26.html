Tadamon massacre prime suspect Amjad Yousef arrested in Hama Ghab Plain operation

Interior Minister Anas Khattab announced the capture of Amjad Yousef, the former Branch 227 intelligence officer filmed personally killing 41 civilians in the 2013 Tadamon massacre, in a tracked operation across the Ghab Plain in rural Hama. Yousef had been in hiding since the December 2024 fall of the regime, and his arrest is the highest-profile transitional-justice take since Damascus opened the regime accountability file in February.

The new count makes clear the April 8 ceasefire has not extended to the Iranian Kurdish opposition camps inside KRG territory and resets the bar for any meaningful Tehran procedural commitment.
