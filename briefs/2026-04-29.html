Iraq

Apr 28, 2026 · Energy

UAE notifies OPEC and OPEC+ of May 1 exit, Iraq inherits the second-producer chair inside the cartel

The UAE formally notified OPEC and OPEC+ that it will leave both groupings effective May 1, ending nearly 60 years of membership and removing the cartel's third-largest producer at the moment Hormuz transits sit near zero, with ADNOC chief Sultan Al Jaber framing the move as a sovereign decision tied to the 5 million bpd 2027 capacity goal. The exit promotes Iraq into the second-largest OPEC producer behind Saudi Arabia and reshapes every quota and compensation-cut conversation Baghdad has been running through Vienna since the February shut-in began.

Trump declares Iran in "state of collapse," demands immediate Hormuz reopening as Tehran proposal sits on his desk

President Trump posted that Iran has "informed us" it is in a "state of collapse" and is asking the US to lift the Hormuz naval blockade "as soon as possible," with Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent separately writing that Iran's oil industry is "creaking" and would soon be unable to pump, while Tehran's military rebutted that the ceasefire is "battle silence" and the country is ready for prolonged conflict. The exchange resets the political read on the April 27 Tehran offer to lift its closure of the strait in return for an end to the US blockade and is the first Trump-side framing that explicitly conditions Hormuz reopening on Iranian capitulation rather than on a nuclear-track linkage.
