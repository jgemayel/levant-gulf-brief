Iraq

May 3, 2026 · Energy High impact

OPEC+ clears a 188,000 bpd June hike in its first meeting without the UAE, Iraq inside the residual seven

The seven-country OPEC+ core of Algeria, Iraq, Kazakhstan, Kuwait, Oman, Russia and Saudi Arabia met virtually Sunday and approved a third consecutive monthly quota hike of 188,000 barrels per day for June, the first such call since the UAE formally exited on May 1. The decision was framed as largely symbolic with Hormuz still closed, designed to signal that the cartel will be ready to lift supply once the war stops, with Saudi Arabia's quota set to 10.291 million bpd against an actual March output of 7.76 million bpd. Brent hovered near a four-year high of USD 125 a barrel into the call.

May 3, 2026 · Security High impact

Trump puts Project Freedom in motion from Monday, US Navy stands up a Hormuz escort posture with destroyers, 100 aircraft and 15,000 personnel

Trump announced Project Freedom on Sunday, with the US Navy beginning to escort neutral-flag ships through the Strait of Hormuz starting Monday morning Middle East time and operating with guided-missile destroyers, more than 100 land and sea-based aircraft and drones, and roughly 15,000 service members deployed in the vicinity. Two US officials told reporters Navy ships would be "in the vicinity" rather than physically alongside commercial hulls, with Trump warning that any interference will be "dealt with forcefully." Tehran called the mission a violation of the standing ceasefire.
